# VETASSESS — Knowledge Base

## Overview

VETASSESS (Vocational Education and Training Assessment Services) is
Australia's largest skills assessing authority, designated by the
Department of Home Affairs to assess more than 360 occupations for
skilled-migration visa purposes. VETASSESS covers two streams:
**Professional and General occupations** (341 occupations including
marketing specialists, management consultants, external auditors,
early childhood teachers, university lecturers) and **Trade
occupations** (27 trades including cooks, chefs, motor mechanics,
electricians, welders). A VETASSESS outcome is required for most 189,
190, 491, 186, 482, and 485 visa pathways where the nominated ANZSCO
occupation sits outside the scope of ACS, Engineers Australia, CPA,
CA ANZ, or the other specialist bodies.

## Eligibility Rules

A VETASSESS assessment evaluates **qualification comparability**
against the AQF and **employment experience** at an appropriate skill
level that is "highly relevant" to the nominated occupation.

### Professional and General occupations

VETASSESS classifies every professional occupation into one of five
qualification groups (A through E). The group is per-occupation and
published on each occupation's information sheet.

- **Group A**: ANZSCO skill level 1. Bachelor or higher in a
  highly-relevant field plus 1+ year post-qualification
  highly-relevant employment in the last 5 years. Example:
  University Lecturer (242111).
- **Group B**: ANZSCO skill level 1. Bachelor or higher; experience
  scales across Pathways 1 to 4. Example: Marketing Specialist
  (225113), Management Consultant (224711 / 224713).
- **Group C**: ANZSCO skill level 2. Diploma or higher in a
  highly-relevant field plus post-qualification experience.
  Example: Cafe or Restaurant Manager (141111).
- **Group D**: ANZSCO skill level 2 or 3. Certificate IV or higher,
  offset by longer experience.
- **Group E**: ANZSCO skill level 3+, qualification not in a
  highly-relevant field. Longest employment requirement.

### Professional occupation pathways

Most Group B occupations offer four employment pathways (20+ hours
per week; only months in the last 10 years are typically counted):

- **Pathway 1**: 1+ year post-qualification highly-relevant
  employment in the last 5 years.
- **Pathway 2**: 2+ years post-qualification highly-relevant.
- **Pathway 3**: 3+ years post-qualification highly-relevant
  (typical where the Bachelor is not in the highly-relevant field).
- **Pathway 4**: 6+ years total including 1+ year highly-relevant
  in the last 5 years (typical where the qualification field is
  unrelated).

### Trade occupations (TSA)

Trade applicants go through the Trades Skills Assessment, not
Professional assessment. Two pathways:

- **Pathway 1 (3-stage)**: Stage 1 Documentary Evidence, Stage 2
  Technical Interview, Stage 3 Practical Assessment. Used for most
  overseas-qualified tradespeople.
- **Pathway 2 (2-stage)**: Stage 1 Documentary, Stage 2 Technical
  Interview. Used where a practical is not required.

Applicants must typically show a qualification equivalent to the
relevant Australian Certificate III plus 3+ years of
post-qualification employment in the nominated trade.

### English

VETASSESS does not conduct English tests and does not set a mandatory
English score for Professional assessments. The Home Affairs English
threshold still applies at visa stage.

## Required Documents

- **Passport bio page (mandatory)**. Colour scan of photo and
  signature pages.
- **Passport photograph (mandatory)**. Recent colour photo to
  Australian passport standards.
- **Three identity documents (mandatory)**. National ID, birth
  certificate, driver licence, marriage certificate (any three).
  NAATI-certified translation for non-English documents.
- **CV (mandatory)**. Chronological full employment history, dated,
  aligned with the Statements of Service.
- **Qualification award certificates (mandatory)**. Certified colour
  copies of each degree, diploma, or certificate relied on; certified
  English translation for non-English awards.
- **Academic transcripts (mandatory)**. Full mark sheets showing
  subjects and grades.
- **Statement of Service (mandatory per role)**. One signed letter
  on company letterhead per employer covering job title, dates,
  hours, leave, duties, signatory details. See Letter Template.
- **Third-party evidence of employment (mandatory per role)**. At
  least two of: payslips, tax returns (Form 16 India, P60 UK, W-2
  US), bank statements showing salary credits, employer tax
  certificates, superannuation or PF statements. Must cover the
  whole claimed period.
- **Organisational chart (conditional)**. Required for management
  and senior-professional occupations; must show the applicant's
  position, direct reports, and reporting line up.
- **Project list (conditional)**. Required for management
  consultants, marketing specialists, and similar: client, scope,
  duration, applicant's role.
- **Research publications (conditional)**. Mandatory for University
  Lecturer (242111): at least one peer-reviewed paper in the last
  5 years; in-progress, submitted, or under-review do not count.
- **Registration or licence (conditional)**. Required for regulated
  occupations (teaching, healthcare).

## Letter Template

The VETASSESS equivalent of a reference letter is a **Statement of
Service** (also called Statement of Employment). One statement per
employer, on original company letterhead, signed and dated. Required
blocks:

1. **Employer identification**. Legal entity name, ABN (Australian)
   or local business registration number, registered address,
   phone, website.
2. **Employee identification**. Applicant's full legal name exactly
   as on passport.
3. **Employment dates**. Start and end dates (or "ongoing") in
   dd/mm/yyyy. Breaks, unpaid leave, and role changes itemised.
4. **Position title(s)**. Exact title(s); multiple titles broken
   into sub-periods.
5. **Hours per week**. Explicit weekly hours. VETASSESS requires
   20+ hours per week for a role to count.
6. **Paid leave and salary**. Annual leave entitlement and salary
   or salary band.
7. **Detailed duties**. 8 to 12 duty statements mapped to the
   nominated ANZSCO's key duties. Each begins with an action verb
   and references tools, scale, stakeholders, outcomes. Generic
   phrasing ("handled marketing") will be queried or rejected.
8. **Signatory block**. Full name, position, direct phone, direct
   company email (not personal gmail), signature. Must be direct
   supervisor, HR manager, or company director. Peer and
   self-signatures are rejected.

If the employer has folded or refuses, a Statutory Declaration plus
independent evidence (payslips, tax, client engagement letters) may
substitute, but this path is high-risk and frequently queried.

## Duty Descriptors

Duty language in the Statement of Service must closely mirror the
ANZSCO descriptor for the nominated occupation. VETASSESS compares
duties line-by-line; duties that skew toward a different ANZSCO code
trigger either an alternative-occupation outcome (which can break a
189/190/491 pathway) or a negative outcome.

### Marketing Specialist (225113)

Group B, level 1. Highly-relevant fields: Marketing, Advertising,
Business Communications, Public Relations (Business Management,
Business, Commerce are case-by-case). Duties: identifying market
opportunities; advising on product mix, pricing, advertising and
sales promotion, selling and distribution channels; developing
marketing strategy and campaigns; market research; analysing
campaign performance; managing agency and media relationships.

### Management Consultant (224711 / 224713)

Group B, level 1. Bachelor or higher in Business Management or
Organisation Management. Duties: independent, objective consultancy
on management topics; researching and analysing client needs;
formulating recommendations on client business and organisational
problems; assisting with strategy, organisational analysis,
implementation.

### External Auditor (221213)

Group B, level 1 (verify on the live occupation sheet). Bachelor
or higher in Accounting or closely related; CA/CPA not mandatory
for VETASSESS but typically required at visa stage. Duties:
planning and conducting audits of financial statements; examining
internal controls; verifying accounting records; reporting audit
findings to management and audit committees; ensuring compliance
with accounting standards.

### Chef (351311)

Trade Skills Assessment. Australian Certificate IV in Commercial
Cookery or equivalent; typically 3+ years post-qualification.
Duties: planning menus; estimating food and labour costs; ordering
supplies; overseeing food quality and presentation; training and
supervising kitchen staff; ensuring food safety and hygiene
compliance.

### Cook (351411)

Trade Skills Assessment. Certificate III in Commercial Cookery or
equivalent; typically 3+ years post-qualification. Duties:
preparing, seasoning, and cooking food; portioning dishes;
following recipes and kitchen procedures; maintaining kitchen
hygiene; working under the direction of a chef.

### Cafe or Restaurant Manager (141111)

Group C, level 2. AQF Diploma or higher in Hospitality Management
or Food and Beverage Services (General Management or Tourism-only
not accepted). Duties: planning menus with chefs; arranging
purchasing and pricing; selecting, training, and supervising
waiting and kitchen staff; rostering and performance management;
ensuring compliance with health and liquor regulations.

### Mechanical Engineering Draftsperson (312511)

Group C, level 2 (verify on the live occupation sheet). AQF
Associate Degree, Advanced Diploma, or Diploma in a mechanical or
drafting field. Duties: preparing detailed drawings and plans of
mechanical equipment from engineer specs; producing 3D CAD models
(SolidWorks, AutoCAD, Inventor); updating drawings for design
changes; producing manufacturing drawings and bills of material.

### Early Childhood Teacher (241111)

Group A, level 1; typically requires registration. Bachelor or
higher in Early Childhood Education. Duties: planning and
implementing educational programs for children below school age;
assessing child development and learning outcomes; liaising with
parents and other professionals; ensuring compliance with early
childhood regulations.

### University Lecturer (242111)

Group A, level 1. Bachelor or higher (Master or Doctoral strongly
preferred) in a discipline directly relevant to the teaching and
research specialisation. Research requirement: at least 1
peer-reviewed publication in the last 5 years; in-progress,
submitted, or under-review do not count. Duties: delivering
lectures, tutorials, seminars, lab sessions; designing and
updating curriculum; grading; supervising honours and postgraduate
students; conducting independent research and publishing in
refereed journals.

### Accountant (General) (221111)

Primarily assessed by CPA Australia, CA ANZ, or IPA, not
VETASSESS. Include in VETASSESS drafting only when the user has
confirmed a non-standard pathway (e.g. 485 Graduate assessment or
Points Test Advice). Otherwise direct to the correct accounting
body.

## Fees & Submission Mechanics

Fees as published by VETASSESS, effective 22 October 2025, in AUD.

- **Priority Processing** (additional): 907.50 / 825.00. Processed
  in 10 business days after eligibility is confirmed.
- **Points Test Advice only**: 342.10 / 311.00.
- **Subclass 485 Standard**: 457.60 / 416.00; Review 291.50 /
  265.00; Change of Occupation 416.90 / 379.00.
- **Review of Outcome** (lodge within 90 days): qualification only
  374.00 / 340.00; employment only 669.90 / 609.00; both 1,004.30

  745.00.
- **Appeal**: 1,190.20 / 1,082.00.
- **Reissue of Outcome Letter**: 82.50 / 75.00.
- **Renewal** (within 3 years): 520.30 / 473.00.

### Trade (no GST split published)

- **Pathway 1**: Stage 1 Documentary 1,120.00; Stage 2 Technical
  Interview 2,000.00; Stage 3 Practical 2,200.00.
- **Pathway 2**: Stage 1 1,120.00; Stage 2 900.00.
- **Reassessment**: Stage 1 450.00; Stage 2 (Pathway 1) 1,000.00;
  Stage 3 1,100.00.
- **Review (per stage)**: 700.00.

### Submission mechanics

- **Portal**: VETASSESS Online Services at
  https://online.vetassess.com.au. Postal not accepted.
- **Payment**: credit card at submission.
- **File formats**: PDF, JPG, PNG. Per-document-slot upload. See
  current VETASSESS guidance for the live per-file size limit.
- **Bundling**: documents uploaded per-slot against the online
  form, not as a zip. Each Statement of Service is a single PDF
  per employer.

## Common Risks & Watchpoints

- **Qualification-field mismatch**. Group B and C assume a highly
  relevant qualification field. A Bachelor in an adjacent field
  (e.g. Business Management for Marketing Specialist) can push the
  applicant to Pathway 3 or 4, requiring 3 to 6 years instead of
  1 to 2.
- **Weekly hours below 20**. Any role under 20 hours per week is
  struck from the experience tally. The Statement of Service must
  state hours explicitly; do not rely on inferred full-time.
- **Payment evidence gaps**. Missing payslips or tax documents for
  any segment of claimed employment trigger an RFI or a partial
  deduction. Gap months are treated as unemployment unless
  documented.
- **Duties drift to a different ANZSCO**. A Marketing Specialist
  whose duties read as Advertising Manager (131114) or Public
  Relations Manager (131115) gets reassessed to that occupation,
  which may break visa eligibility. Align duties to the nominated
  ANZSCO before drafting.
- **Org chart missing for management roles**. For Management
  Consultant, Cafe or Restaurant Manager, and similar, the org
  chart is effectively mandatory. Submissions without one
  frequently get deducted to a non-management alternative.
- **Statement signed by peer or applicant**. Rejected. Signatory
  must be a direct supervisor, department head, HR manager, or
  company director.
- **Research publication claim unbacked**. University Lecturer
  applicants who claim research without a published peer-reviewed
  paper from the last 5 years get a negative outcome.
- **Trade applicants skipping payment evidence**. Stage 1
  applicants relying solely on employer letters routinely fail.

## Post-Submission Timeline

- **Standard Professional Skills Assessment**: 7 weeks from
  complete submission. Extends where documents are incomplete or
  VETASSESS raises an RFI. Per the current processing times page
  (retrieved 2026-04-23; no explicit last-updated timestamp).
- **Priority Processing (Professional)**: 10 business days after
  eligibility is confirmed. Not available for trade.
- **Trade Skills Assessment**: no fixed window; depends on the
  specific trade program and completeness of evidence.
- **Reassessment (within 12 months)**: 7 weeks.
- **Review of Outcome**: 7 weeks.
- **Appeal**: 12 weeks.
- **Communication channel**: VETASSESS Online Services portal plus
  email. RFIs appear as portal tasks.
- **RFI response window**: typically 28 days. Failure to respond
  triggers assessment on documents already submitted (usually
  negative or insufficient).
- **Refund policy**: partial refund only if withdrawn before
  assessment commences. Administration fees retained.
