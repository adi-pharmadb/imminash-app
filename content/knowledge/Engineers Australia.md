# Engineers Australia — Knowledge Base

## Overview

Engineers Australia (EA) is the designated skills assessing authority
for engineering occupations under the Australian skilled migration
program. The Migration Skills Assessment (MSA) evaluates whether an
applicant's qualifications, English proficiency, and (where required)
demonstrated competencies meet the entry-to-practice standard for the
nominated occupation. Applicants lodge an MSA for 189, 190, 491, 186,
482, or 485 visas across four EA categories: Professional Engineer,
Engineering Technologist, Engineering Associate, Engineering Manager.

## Eligibility Rules

EA recognises five assessment pathways across two tracks. The track is
determined by the qualification; the pathway determines whether a CDR is
required.

**Accord Track (no CDR required)**

- **Australian Qualification**: Bachelor or higher engineering degree
  accredited by EA, commenced during the accreditation period. From 1
  Sept 2024, only EA-accredited qualifications (not RTO-delivered) are
  eligible at the Advanced Diploma and Associate Degree levels.
- **Washington Accord**: 4-year Professional Engineer qualification from
  a signatory, within the recognition period (includes CTI-accredited
  French qualifications).
- **Sydney Accord**: Engineering Technologist qualification from a
  signatory.
- **Dublin Accord**: Engineering Associate qualification from a
  signatory.

**CDR Track**

- **Competency Demonstration Report (CDR)**: required where the
  qualification is non-accredited, provisionally accredited, from a
  non-signatory, does not match the nominated occupational category, or
  is a combined/articulated qualification. Applicant produces 3 Career
  Episodes, Summary Statement, and CPD log mapped to the Stage 1
  Competency Standard for the nominated category.

**English Language (mandatory under BOTH tracks)**

- IELTS Academic or General: 6.0 minimum in each of L/R/W/S (per-band
  floor, not overall).
- PTE Academic: 50 min in each skill. TOEFL iBT: L12, R13, W21, S18.
  CAE: 169 per band.
- Exemptions: English-taught qualification from AU, UK, US, CA, NZ, IE;
  or passport holder of UK, IE, US, CA, NZ.
- Tests valid within 2 years of lodgement.

**Skilled Employment**: optional Employment Review add-on, required
only when claiming points for skilled employment (189/190/491) or when
EA must nominate a skilled employment start date.

## Required Documents

- **Passport biodata page (mandatory)**. Colour scan; used to match
  applicant name across all documents.
- **Current CV (mandatory)**. Chronological, full education and
  employment history. Dates must reconcile with employment evidence.
- **Qualification documents (mandatory)**. Certified colour copies of
  degree certificate(s) AND academic transcripts for every engineering
  qualification claimed. Transcripts list every subject with grades.
  Non-English documents require a NAATI (or country-equivalent)
  certified translation alongside the original.
- **English test report (mandatory unless exempt)**. IELTS, PTE, TOEFL
  iBT, or CAE less than 2 years old. Include the test taker
  receipt/reference for online verification.
- **CDR (mandatory on CDR pathway only)**. Three Career Episodes +
  Summary Statement + CPD log. See Letter Template.
- **Employment evidence (mandatory if claiming employment or using
  Employment Review)**. Per role: reference letter on company
  letterhead with position title, dates (dd/mm/yyyy), hours/week,
  salary, duties. Plus payslips (first, last, annual samples), tax
  statements, bank statements showing salary deposits, or
  superannuation/social-security contributions as corroboration.
- **Statutory declaration (conditional)**. Where an employer reference
  is unobtainable (business closed, supervisor uncontactable,
  self-employment). Sworn before a JP, notary, or equivalent, with
  corroborating financial evidence.
- **Change of name evidence (conditional)**. Marriage certificate or
  deed poll if the name on qualifications differs from the passport.
- **Professional body memberships (optional)**. Strengthen the case.

## Letter Template

**The EA "letter template" is the Competency Demonstration Report
(CDR), not an employer reference letter.** Accord pathway applicants do
not produce a CDR. CDR pathway applicants produce all three artefacts
below, authored in first person and mapped to the Stage 1 Competency
Standard for the nominated category (PE, ET, or EA).

**1. Career Episodes (three required)**. Each describes a discrete
project of engineering activity the applicant personally performed.
Structure: Introduction (approx 100 words: dates, location, org,
position); Background (200 to 500 words: project, objectives,
applicant's work area, reporting line); Personal Engineering Activity
(500 to 1500 words: what the applicant personally did, knowledge/skills
applied, problems solved, contribution); Summary (50 to 100 words).
Total 1000 to 2500 words per episode. Written in first person singular
("I designed"), never team voice ("we"). Paragraphs numbered 1.1, 1.2
(Episode 1); 2.1 (Episode 2); 3.1 (Episode 3) so the Summary Statement
can cross-reference them. At least one episode should be from the last
3 years. Collectively the three must evidence every Stage 1 competency
element for the nominated category.

**2. Summary Statement (one required)**. Tabular cross-reference
mapping each Stage 1 element (PE1.1 through PE3.6, or ET/EA equivalent)
to the specific Career Episode paragraph(s) where it was demonstrated.
EA publishes a template per category. Every element must have at least
one paragraph reference; gaps are the most common cause of
insufficient-competency outcomes.

**3. CPD log (one required)**. List of formal and informal continuing
professional development since graduation: conferences, short courses,
workshops, private technical study, paper preparation, industry body
participation. Format: date, activity, provider, duration, type.
Typically one page; must show ongoing engagement, not a single burst.

**Signatories**: the CDR is authored and signed by the applicant only.
Employer reference letters supporting the Employment Review add-on
must be on company letterhead, signed by a direct supervisor or HR,
stating title, dates, hours/week, salary, duties.

## Duty Descriptors

Duty language for each Career Episode and for any employer reference
letter should map to the Stage 1 Competency Standard for the nominated
category AND to the ANZSCO descriptor for the target code. PE, ET, and
EA share a 3-cluster structure (Knowledge/Skill Base, Engineering
Application Ability, Professional/Personal Attributes), differentiated
by depth and autonomy.

### Professional Engineer (PE)

- **PE1 Knowledge and Skill Base**: PE1.1 theory-based understanding of
  natural/physical sciences and engineering fundamentals; PE1.2
  conceptual advanced mathematics, numerical analysis, statistics,
  computer/information sciences; PE1.3 in-depth specialist body of
  knowledge; PE1.4 discernment of knowledge development and research
  directions; PE1.5 contextual factors (social, cultural, environmental,
  economic); PE1.6 professional and business practice.
- **PE2 Engineering Application Ability**: PE2.1 established methods for
  complex problem solving; PE2.2 fluent use of techniques, tools,
  resources; PE2.3 systematic synthesis and design; PE2.4 systematic
  conduct and management of engineering projects.
- **PE3 Professional/Personal Attributes**: PE3.1 ethical conduct and
  professional accountability; PE3.2 effective oral/written
  communication; PE3.3 creative, innovative, proactive demeanour; PE3.4
  professional use and management of information; PE3.5 orderly
  self-management; PE3.6 team membership and leadership.

### Engineering Technologist (ET)

- **ET1**: ET1.1 systematic theory-based understanding of sciences and
  fundamentals; ET1.2 mathematics/numerical analysis/statistics and
  computing supporting technology practice; ET1.3 in-depth specialist
  knowledge; ET1.4 discernment of developments in practice area; ET1.5
  contextual factors; ET1.6 professional and business practice.
- **ET2**: ET2.1 established methods for broadly defined engineering
  problems; ET2.2 fluent use of techniques, tools, resources; ET2.3
  synthesis and design for broadly defined problems; ET2.4 systematic
  project management.
- **ET3**: ET3.1 to ET3.6 mirror PE3.1 to PE3.6 at the Technologist
  responsibility level.

### Engineering Associate (EA)

- **EA1**: EA1.1 descriptive, formula-based understanding of sciences
  and fundamentals; EA1.2 procedure-based mathematics and computing;
  EA1.3 in-depth practical knowledge in specialist sub-disciplines;
  EA1.4 discernment of developments; EA1.5 contextual factors; EA1.6
  scope, principles, norms, and bounds of sustainable practice.
- **EA2**: EA2.1 established methods for well-defined engineering
  problems; EA2.2 techniques, tools, resources; EA2.3 synthesis and
  design for well-defined problems; EA2.4 systematic project management.
- **EA3**: EA3.1 to EA3.6 mirror PE3.1 to PE3.6 at the Associate
  responsibility level.

### Engineering Manager

Separate stream assessed on demonstrated engineering management
experience. Applicants must hold a Professional Engineer-level
qualification AND demonstrate multi-year management responsibility
(team leadership, budget accountability, strategic engineering
decisions).

## Fees & Submission Mechanics

All fees AUD, current as of 2026-04-23. Inc-GST is the amount charged.

**Assessment fees (ex-GST / inc-GST)**

- International Accords (Washington/Sydney/Dublin) basic: $505 / $555.50;
  +Employment Review $940 / $1,034; +PhD $780 / $858; +both $1,215 /
  $1,336.50.
- Australian Accredited basic: $315 / $346.50; +Employment Review $750 /
  $825; +PhD $590 / $649; +both $1,025 / $1,127.50.
- CDR standard: $940 / $1,034; +Employment Review $1,375 / $1,512.50;
  +PhD $1,215 / $1,336.50; +both $1,650 / $1,815.

**Add-ons**: Fast-track $360 / $396 (non-refundable; 20 business days to
assignment). Employment Review only $485 / $533.50. PhD only $325 /
$357.50. Review $335 / $368.50. Appeal $640 / $704. Updated outcome
letter $165 / $181.50. Admin fee $130 / $143.

**Portal**: EA's online MSA system at
https://www.engineersaustralia.org.au/migrants/migration-skills-assessment.
Fully electronic; postal submission not accepted.

**Files**: PDF per document slot. Colour, legible, complete. Each
artefact (certificate, transcript, Career Episode, Summary Statement,
CPD, reference letter) uploads discretely.

**Payment**: credit/debit card at submission. Non-refundable once
assessment begins.

## Common Risks & Watchpoints

- **Plagiarism in the CDR is permanent**. EA screens every CDR with
  automated tools and assessor review. Copying from other applicants'
  CDRs, online sample CDRs, EA standards documents, or published
  literature without attribution results in refusal AND a permanent ban
  from future EA assessment. Highest-stakes risk on the CDR pathway.
  Every sentence must be the applicant's own words about their own work.
- **Team voice in Career Episodes**. EA requires first-person singular
  because the CDR assesses the individual. "We designed" episodes are
  returned or fail competency mapping.
- **Competency gaps in the Summary Statement**. Every Stage 1 element
  must cross-reference at least one Career Episode paragraph. A single
  unreferenced element (e.g. PE3.1 ethics, PE3.5 self-management)
  triggers an insufficient-competency outcome.
- **Category mismatch**. A 3-year Bachelor of Technology usually maps
  to Engineering Technologist (Sydney Accord), not Professional
  Engineer (Washington Accord). Verify AQF-equivalent level first.
- **English test**. IELTS Academic and General Training both accepted;
  per-band 6.0 floor (not overall). Valid for 2 years.
- **Employment evidence inconsistent with CV**. Employment Review
  line-by-line checks references against payslips, tax documents, and
  CV. Mismatches in dates, hours, or title trigger an RFI and can
  exclude the period from the skilled employment start date.
- **Wrong Stage 1 standard**. PE, ET, EA each have their own Stage 1
  Standard. A CDR written to PE but lodged as ET is a systematic fail.
- **Misreading the 15-week SLA**. 15 weeks is time to assessor
  assignment, not to outcome. Real time-to-outcome is 15 weeks plus
  assessor review plus any RFI cycles.

## Post-Submission Timeline

- **Assignment to assessor**: ~15 weeks from complete submission
  (standard); 20 business days under fast-track (assignment, not
  outcome).
- **Channel**: EA portal for all assessor correspondence and RFIs; email
  notification to the registered address. Phone for procedural only.
- **RFI**: assessor may request extra docs, clarifications, or rewritten
  Career Episodes. Typically 28 days to respond. Non-response can close
  or finalise on available evidence.
- **Outcome**: portal-issued letter stating nominated category, ANZSCO
  code, and (if Employment Review included) recognised skilled
  employment start date. Unsuccessful outcomes state reasons.
- **Review**: lodge within 3 months of outcome. Fee $357.50 inc-GST.
  ~7 weeks. Fee refunded on success with revised outcome letter.
- **Appeal**: only after completed review. Lodge within 6 months of
  original outcome. Fee $704 inc-GST. Up to 12 weeks, independent
  panel. Both fees refunded on successful appeal. Decisions final.
- **Refund**: fast-track component non-refundable once paid; main fee
  non-refundable once assessment has commenced. Partial refund only if
  withdrawn before assessor allocation, less $143 inc-GST admin fee.
