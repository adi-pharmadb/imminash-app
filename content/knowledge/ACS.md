# ACS — Knowledge Base

## Overview

The Australian Computer Society (ACS) is the designated skills assessing
authority for Information and Communications Technology (ICT) occupations
under the Australian skilled migration program. ACS assesses whether an
applicant's qualifications and ICT employment experience meet the standard
required for their nominated ANZSCO occupation. Most applicants for ACS
assessment are on 482, 485, or 186/189/190/491 pathways targeting ICT roles.

## Eligibility Rules

ACS recognises four assessment pathways, each with different minimum
experience requirements based on the applicant's qualification:

- **ICT Major pathway**: Bachelor degree or higher with a major in ICT.
  Requires 2 years of closely related post-qualification ICT experience,
  OR 4 years of related ICT experience (not limited to post-qualification).
- **ICT Minor pathway**: Bachelor degree or higher with an ICT minor.
  Requires 5 years of closely related post-qualification ICT experience,
  OR 6 years of related ICT experience.
- **Non-ICT Qualification pathway**: Bachelor degree or higher in a non-ICT
  field. Requires 6 years of closely related ICT work experience.
- **No Qualification pathway**: No tertiary qualification. Requires 8 years
  of closely related ICT work experience, with the last 4 years in the
  nominated ANZSCO occupation.

A Professional Year completion in Australia reduces the onshore experience
requirement by one year for graduates of Australian institutions.

"Closely related" means duties aligned with the ANZSCO unit group
descriptor for the nominated occupation. "Related" means duties aligned
with the broader ICT sector but not specifically the nominated occupation.

## Required Documents

- **Employment Reference (mandatory)**. Employer reference letter on
  company letterhead detailing ICT duties, start and end dates (dd/mm/yyyy),
  and weekly hours. Signed by a direct supervisor or HR manager. One letter
  per employer. Each role should include 6 to 8 ICT-specific duty
  statements. Optional but strongly preferred: include employer ABN/ACN
  and supervisor contact details.
- **CV or Résumé (mandatory)**. Detailed CV showing full employment history
  with ICT focus, chronologically ordered, with dates consistent with the
  employment references.
- **Qualification Documents (mandatory)**. Certified copies of all degree
  certificates and academic transcripts. For non-English documents, include
  a NAATI-certified translation.
- **Statutory Declaration (conditional)**. Required only where the user is
  self-employed or where an employer reference is unavailable (e.g.
  employer has folded, supervisor cannot be contacted). Must be signed by
  a JP or equivalent witness in the country of execution.
- **RPL Report (conditional)**. Recognition of Prior Learning report,
  required only for applicants without an ICT qualification pursuing the
  "No Qualification" pathway. Structured under the ACS RPL Form.

## Letter Template

Each employment reference must be signed and dated on company letterhead
and must cover the following blocks in order:

1. **Company identification**: legal entity name, ABN/ACN, company
   address, phone number, website.
2. **Employee identification**: applicant's full legal name matching their
   passport.
3. **Role details**: exact job title, start date (dd/mm/yyyy), end date (or
   "current"), weekly hours (full-time = 35+ hours/week).
4. **Duties list**: 6 to 8 ICT-specific duty statements, each beginning
   with an ANZSCO action verb (Designed, Developed, Implemented, Analysed,
   Led, Maintained, Tested, Documented). Each duty must be specific enough
   to demonstrate skill-level 1-3 work (tools used, measurable outcomes,
   stakeholder context, team size where relevant). Vague phrasing like
   "worked on systems" or "helped the team" fails.
5. **Sign-off**: signatory's full name, job title, direct phone, direct
   email, signature. The signatory must be a direct supervisor or HR
   manager; a peer cannot sign.

The reference letter is the single most scrutinised document in an ACS
application. Every assertion in the duty list should be one the user can
substantiate verbally if the assessor follows up with the employer.

## Duty Descriptors

Duties must clearly demonstrate ICT content at the nominated skill level.
At least **65%** of a role's duties must be ICT-related for that role's
months to count toward the experience requirement.

ACS recognises the following ICT duty categories:

- Software development and programming
- Systems analysis and design
- Database administration and management
- Network engineering and administration
- ICT project management
- ICT security and cyber security
- Web development and multimedia
- ICT support and testing

Per-occupation duty descriptors are published under each ANZSCO unit group
by ABS and mirrored in the ACS ANZSCO Code Information Guide. The agent
should use the ANZSCO descriptor for the nominated occupation as the
starting template for duty generation, then specialise to the user's
actual tooling, scope, and outcomes.

## Fees & Submission Mechanics

- **Application fee**: AUD 500 (post-Australian-Qualification assessment)
  to AUD 1,050 (skills assessment with RPL), depending on pathway. As of
  2025. Fees are payable by card via the ACS online portal.
- **Submission portal**: https://www.acs.org.au/msa/online-application.html
- **File format**: PDF, individual upload per document, max 3MB per file.
- **Payment**: credit or debit card at submission. No postal submissions
  accepted for the standard Skills Assessment.
- **Withdrawal / refund**: partial refund available only if withdrawn
  before assessment begins (typically within 14 days of submission).

## Common Risks & Watchpoints

- **Duty list too vague**. Assessors reject references where duties read
  as generic descriptions ("worked with databases", "developed software")
  without specifying tools, scope, or outcomes. Every duty must pass a
  "could a non-ICT reader tell this is skill-level 1-3 work?" test.
- **Hours below 20/week**. ACS does not count any role where weekly hours
  are below 20. Casual engagements under 20 hours/week get struck from
  the experience tally.
- **Qualification-experience misalignment**. ACS deducts 2 to 4 years from
  the experience tally when the pathway mapping is imperfect (e.g. ICT
  Minor degree where the applicant is claiming the ICT Major pathway).
  The user should NOT be promised the full experience count without
  accounting for possible deduction.
- **Short gaps treated as continuous employment**. If the gap between two
  employers is longer than 4 weeks, don't present it as continuous
  employment in the narrative. Assessors check dates against the CV and
  mismatches trigger a request for more information.
- **Reference letter signed by a peer**. Letters signed by anyone other
  than a direct supervisor or HR are routinely rejected; letters signed
  by the applicant themselves are always rejected.
- **ANZSCO code mismatch**. Duties that fit one ANZSCO better than the
  nominated one will cause ACS to propose a different code, which may
  break the 189/190/491 visa pathway. Verify ANZSCO alignment before
  drafting duties, not after.

## Post-Submission Timeline

- **Typical assessment duration**: 10 to 12 weeks from complete submission.
  ACS publishes current turnaround times on their website.
- **Communication channel**: the ACS online portal and the email
  registered on the application. All assessor requests appear as portal
  tasks; email is a secondary notification.
- **Request for more information (RFMI)**: if ACS raises questions, the
  applicant has 28 days to respond. The clock on the original assessment
  pauses until the RFMI is addressed.
- **Outcome letter**: issued via the portal. Outcome is one of Positive
  (with nominated ANZSCO + start date of skilled employment), Positive
  (with alternative ANZSCO), Insufficient (deduction means experience
  doesn't meet threshold), or Negative.
