# TRA — Knowledge Base

## Overview

Trades Recognition Australia (TRA) is the skills assessing authority
operated by the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations for
traditional trade occupations on the ANZSCO Skilled Occupation Lists.
TRA compares an applicant's VET qualification and paid employment
against the Australian training package underpinning each trade and
issues a skills assessment used for 189, 190, 491, 482, 485, 186 and
494 visas. TRA is the primary assessor for electricians, cooks,
carpenters, motor mechanics, plumbers, welders, bricklayers, cabinet
makers, hairdressers, chefs and similar Cert III / IV trades.

**Important notice**: TRA has paused new registrations for the Offshore
Skills Assessment Program (OSAP) and TSS (subclass 482) Skills
Assessment from 14 March 2026 while the portal is rebuilt. Confirm
current status on the TRA site before any applicant pays a fee. MSA and
JRP remain open.

## Eligibility Rules

TRA runs four programs. Choice depends on whether the applicant holds a
formal qualification, where they are, and which visa they target.

- **Migration Skills Assessment (MSA)**. Dominant GSM pathway. For
  applicants with a qualification comparable to the relevant Australian
  Cert III / IV and relevant paid employment. Used for
  189/190/491/186/494. Minimum: comparable qualification AND at least
  **three years** of relevant paid employment at 38 hrs/week (or
  part-time equivalent) in the last five years, post-qualification, with
  employment in the last 12 months shown.
- **Offshore Skills Assessment Program (OSAP)**. For offshore 186/494
  applicants who lack a comparable qualification but have substantial
  on-the-job experience. Two stages: documentary review then a practical
  interview at an approved offshore centre. **Paused to new
  registrations from 14 March 2026**.
- **Job Ready Program (JRP)**. Four-step onshore pathway, primarily for
  485 graduates of Australian VET qualifications. Steps:
  (1) Provisional Skills Assessment from desk review; (2) Job Ready
  Employment of 1,725 hours paid work over at least 12 months in the
  nominated occupation; (3) Job Ready Workplace Assessment by a
  TRA-endorsed RTO on site; (4) Job Ready Final Assessment. Typically
  **12 to 18 months**. JRP remains open.
- **TSS Skills Assessment (482)**. For 482 applicants where TRA is the
  listed assessor. **Paused from 14 March 2026** alongside OSAP.

English is tested at the visa stage, not here. Supporting documents must
be in English or have a NAATI-certified translation attached.

## Required Documents

TRA applies a stricter evidentiary standard than ACS or VETASSESS.
Employer reference letters alone are rarely sufficient; applicants must
corroborate employment with primary financial records.

- **Identity documents (mandatory)**. Certified passport bio-page, any
  change-of-name documents, recent passport-style photograph.
- **Qualification certificate and transcript (mandatory for MSA and
  JRP)**. Certified copy of the award and full transcript showing units,
  grades, duration, mode of study. Non-English documents need
  NAATI-certified translations attached.
- **RTO / issuing institution contact details (mandatory)**. TRA
  independently verifies qualifications with the issuer.
- **MSA Employer Template (mandatory for MSA)**. One per employer, TRA's
  fillable PDF, signed by a direct supervisor or HR representative. See
  Letter Template below.
- **Payslips (mandatory)**. At least three consecutive payslips per
  employer covering start, mid, end of employment.
- **PAYG summaries / income statements (mandatory, Australian
  employment)**. One per financial year claimed.
- **Superannuation statements (mandatory, Australian employment)**.
  Employer-contribution fund statements. Strongest corroborating
  evidence because they name the paying entity and contribution period.
- **Tax documents (mandatory, Australian employment)**. ATO Notices of
  Assessment or tax return transcripts per financial year claimed.
- **Employment contract (strongly recommended)**. Signed original showing
  position title, weekly hours, commencement date.
- **Offshore employment evidence (mandatory where applicable)**. Tax
  records, social-security / provident-fund statements, bank statements
  showing salary credits, company registration documents. Internal-only
  documents are insufficient.
- **CV (mandatory)**. Chronological, dates matching every employer
  statement and payslip bundle.
- **Licences (mandatory where held)**. For electrical, plumbing and
  gas-fitting, evidence of any overseas or Australian trade licence.

## Letter Template

The canonical deliverable for MSA is the **MSA Employer Template**, a
fillable PDF published by TRA. Unlike ACS, the employment assertion is
captured in a structured form rather than free-form letter; every field
must be populated exactly.

Required fields, in order:

1. **Employer identification**. Legal entity name, trading name if
   different, street address, phone, email, ABN (or offshore company
   registration number).
2. **Applicant identification**. Full legal name matching passport, date
   of birth, position held.
3. **Employment period**. Start and end date (or "ongoing"), dd/mm/yyyy.
4. **Hours of work**. Hours per week. Full-time = 38 hours. Part-time
   must state exact hours; TRA pro-rates the experience tally.
5. **Remuneration**. Gross annual salary or hourly rate and currency.
6. **Duties performed**. Actual duties mapped against performance
   criteria of the relevant training package qualification. Active
   voice, specific enough to show the applicant performed the full scope
   of the trade. Generic phrasing ("assisted senior tradesperson",
   "general duties") is insufficient.
7. **Tools and equipment used**. Tools, machinery and materials used.
   Commonly under-answered; frequent RFMI trigger.
8. **Supervisor sign-off**. Printed name, position, direct phone, direct
   email, date, signature. Signatory must be senior and contactable;
   TRA verifies by phone or email.

Letters on company letterhead are welcome alongside the template but
do not replace it. A template without corroborating payslips, super and
tax records is treated as unsubstantiated and will fail.

## Duty Descriptors

TRA duty descriptors are keyed to the **Australian VET qualification
code** underpinning each ANZSCO occupation, not to the ANZSCO descriptor
alone. The assessor walks the duty list against the performance criteria
of the units of competency inside the relevant training package
qualification (published at training.gov.au). A duty counts only if it
maps to one or more units of competency in the applicable Cert III or IV.

Applicants should use the units of competency in their nominated
qualification as the starting vocabulary, then specialise to the tools,
materials and job contexts they worked in.

### Electrician (ANZSCO 341111)

- **Qualification**: UEE30820 Certificate III in Electrotechnology
  Electrician (or superseded UEE30811, UEE30807).
- **Criteria**: install and terminate low-voltage wiring; commission
  electrical apparatus; test and fault-find single-phase and three-phase
  circuits; install and verify safety and protection devices; read
  electrical drawings; work under AS/NZS 3000 Wiring Rules.
- Licence evidence mandatory; offshore and any Australian state licence
  must be attached.

### Cook (ANZSCO 351411)

- **Qualification**: SIT30821 Certificate III in Commercial Cookery (or
  SIT30816, SIT30813).
- **Criteria**: prepare stocks, sauces and soups; appetisers and salads;
  poultry, meat and seafood dishes; vegetable, fruit, egg and
  farinaceous dishes; desserts; cakes, pastries and breads; apply
  cookery methods; work in a commercial kitchen; apply food safety;
  coordinate cooking operations.
- Distinct from Chef 351311 (Cert IV SIT40521 plus supervisory
  experience).

### Carpenter (ANZSCO 331212)

- **Qualification**: CPC30220 Certificate III in Carpentry (or CPC30211).
- **Criteria**: carry out levelling; read plans and specifications;
  construct wall, floor and roof frames; install flooring systems,
  windows and doors; construct and dismantle formwork for footings and
  slabs; apply construction WHS.

### Motor Mechanic / General (ANZSCO 321211)

- **Qualification**: AUR30620 Certificate III in Light Vehicle
  Mechanical Technology (or AUR30616).
- **Criteria**: inspect and service engines; diagnose and repair
  engines, electrical systems, cooling / lubrication / fuel systems,
  steering / suspension / braking, drivetrains; use workshop tools;
  apply safe working practices.

### Plumber / General (ANZSCO 334111)

- **Qualification**: CPC32420 Certificate III in Plumbing (or CPC32413).
- **Criteria**: install and fit-off sanitary fixtures; install water
  services, sanitary plumbing, stormwater and sub-soil drainage, gas
  services (where gas-fitting stream applies); read plumbing plans; cut
  and join sheet metal, copper and plastic pipework.
- Plumbing licence evidence mandatory.

For other trades, look up the nominated qualification on training.gov.au,
list the core units of competency, and draft duties against the
performance criteria of those units.

## Fees & Submission Mechanics

All fees in AUD and **non-refundable** once assessment has commenced.
Verify on https://www.tradesrecognitionaustralia.gov.au/21-fees before
the applicant pays; TRA updates fees on 1 July each year.

- **MSA**: AUD 1,070 standard. No priority. As of 2025.
- **MSA reassessment / appeal**: AUD 440 within 90 days of a negative
  outcome.
- **OSAP**: see current TRA guidance. Paused from 14 March 2026.
- **JRP**: staged fee structure paid at each step. Registration
  (Provisional Skills Assessment) is the largest payment; workplace and
  final assessment fees follow. Applicants typically budget AUD 3,500 to
  AUD 4,000 across all four steps. See current TRA guidance.
- **TSS (482)**: see current TRA guidance. Paused from 14 March 2026.

Submission mechanics:

- **Portal**: TRA Online Portal via
  https://www.tradesrecognitionaustralia.gov.au/14-how-apply. Migration
  agents can be added as authorised representatives.
- **Payment**: credit or debit card at submission. No postal, cheque or
  bank transfer.
- **File format**: PDF only. Certifier's stamp and signature must be
  legible. Per-document upload, historically around 5 MB per file. No
  password-protected PDFs; rejected at triage.
- **Refunds**: non-refundable once assessment has commenced. Partial
  refund may be available if withdrawn before triage pickup (typically
  a few business days after submission).

## Common Risks & Watchpoints

- **Uncorroborated employment**. Most common cause of a negative
  outcome. A completed template without matching payslips, super and
  tax records is unsubstantiated. Every month claimed should be
  traceable through at least two independent financial records.
- **Duty list too narrow or generic**. Sub-task-only duties (e.g. a
  Carpenter only describing formwork) lead to a finding the applicant
  did not perform the full trade. Duties must span the qualification's
  core units of competency.
- **Signatory not senior or not contactable**. Signatory must be senior
  to the applicant and must respond when TRA contacts them. Peers,
  family members, or departed staff trigger rejection.
- **Qualification not comparable to Australian Cert III / IV**. Shorter,
  narrower or non-accredited overseas courses may fail the comparability
  test. Applicant may need to pivot to OSAP (when open) or JRP after
  enrolling in an Australian qualification.
- **Employment within the last 12 months missing**. MSA requires current
  paid employment; a three-year history from five years ago fails the
  currency test.
- **Part-time hours not pro-rated**. Part-time counts pro-rata against a
  38-hour benchmark; never present part-time as full-time.
- **Licensed-trade applicants without licence evidence**. For
  electricians, plumbers and gas-fitters, missing overseas licence
  evidence is a common RFMI trigger.
- **Password-protected or illegible scans**. Stopped at intake; scan at
  300 dpi and unlock files before upload.
- **Assuming OSAP or TSS is open**. From 14 March 2026 neither accepts
  new registrations. Do not pay a fee for a closed program.

## Post-Submission Timeline

- **MSA**: ~120 days (17 weeks) from complete submission.
- **OSAP**: ~15 weeks when open, including the offshore technical
  interview. Currently paused.
- **JRP**: 12 to 18 months for the full four-step program; at least 12
  months is the Job Ready Employment period.
- **TSS**: ~10 to 12 weeks when open. Currently paused.
- **Communication**: TRA Online Portal with email notifications.
  Assessor requests appear as portal tasks; phone is used only to verify
  signatories.
- **RFMI**: 28 days to respond; the assessment clock pauses while
  outstanding. A second RFMI is rare and usually signals a negative
  outcome.
- **Outcome letter**: via the portal. One of Positive (with nominated
  ANZSCO and recognised skilled employment start date), Positive with
  Qualification Only, Not Successful, or Withdrawn. A Positive outcome is
  valid for **three years** from date of issue for visa purposes.
