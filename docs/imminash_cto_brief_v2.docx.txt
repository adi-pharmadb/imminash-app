01
	Form Flow Changes
Fixes to the 7-step assessment questionnaire
	

1.1  Step Counter Inconsistency
CURRENT STATE — THE PROBLEM
Steps 1–3 display "X of 6" in the top right. Steps 4+ display "X of 7". The total changes mid-flow because Step 3 (Additional Qualifications) is conditionally inserted based on a previous answer. Users notice this and it signals unreliability.


❌  Current (broken)
• Step 1 shows: 1 OF 6
• Step 2 shows: 2 OF 6
• Step 3 appears conditionally
• Step 4 shows: 4 OF 7 ← goalposts moved
	✅  Required (fixed)
• Option A: Count from the start including conditional steps. Grey out steps not yet active.
• Option B: Show only current step number, no total (e.g. "Step 3" not "3 of 7")
• Choose one approach and apply it consistently across all steps.
	

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
• The step counter displays the same total number on every step of the form
• If conditional steps are used, the total either accounts for them from step 1, or no total is shown
• QA: complete the form selecting both paths (with and without additional qualification) and verify counter never changes total mid-flow
	

Priority: P0  |  Effort: S  |  Owner: Frontend


1.2  Loading Screen — Personalised Sequential Steps
CURRENT STATE — THE PROBLEM
All 4 loading steps appear simultaneously as static bullet points with generic copy. This misses an opportunity to build trust and maintain the emotional engagement the user has built up through 7 steps of the form.


❌  Current copy (generic, simultaneous)
• Finding your best occupation matches...
• Checking which assessing body you need...
• Calculating your points score...
• Mapping out your visa options...
• (all 4 appear at once)
	✅  Required copy (personalised, sequential)
• Step 1 (0ms): "Scanning 574 ANZSCO codes for [job title] profiles..."
• Step 2 (+800ms): "Matching your [field of study] to occupation requirements..."
• Step 3 (+1600ms): "Calculating points — age [X], [Y] years Australian experience..."
• Step 4 (+2400ms): "Mapping state nomination eligibility across 8 territories..."
• (each step appears one at a time, using their actual inputs)
	

Data inputs required for personalisation
Loading step
	Form field to inject
	Fallback if blank
	Step 1
	currentJobTitle
	your job title
	Step 2
	fieldOfStudy
	your field of study
	Step 3
	age + australianExperienceYears
	your profile
	Step 4
	No variable needed
	static copy
	

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
• Each loading step appears individually, not all at once
• Timing: ~800ms between each step reveal
• Step copy uses real form data — if job title is 'Business Analyst', it shows 'Business Analyst', not a generic placeholder
• Fallback copy is used if a field is blank — never show undefined or empty brackets
• The skeleton result cards (blurred placeholder cards) become visible after step 4 completes, before results load
	

Priority: P1  |  Effort: S  |  Owner: Frontend

02
	Results Page — Full Redesign
Stage 4 of the user journey — where users decide to convert or leave
	

WHY THIS SECTION MATTERS
The results page is the most important page in the product. It is the first time the user sees value. It must deliver a clear headline result within 3 seconds, give detailed supporting information for those who want it, and guide the user naturally toward the paid next step. Every change in this section serves one of those three goals.
	

2.1  Page Header — Personalised Summary Card
CURRENT STATE — THE PROBLEM
The results page opens directly with a Points Breakdown table. There is no headline summary. The user has just invested 2+ minutes filling in a form and the first thing they see is a data table — not their answer.


REQUIRED — NEW PAGE STRUCTURE (TOP TO BOTTOM)


ELEMENT 1 — PERSONALISED HEADLINE
[FirstName]'s PR Eligibility Report
Based on official ANZSCO occupation lists, invitation rounds, and DHA points tables.
	ELEMENT 2 — ONE-SENTENCE RESULT SUMMARY
You have 90 points, 3 occupation matches, and are eligible in 6 states.  ✓ Above the 65-point threshold.
	ELEMENT 3 — 4 ANCHOR NAVIGATION BOXES (CLICKABLE, JUMP TO SECTION)
[ Points Breakdown ]   [ #1 ICT Business Analyst ]   [ #2 Database Admin ]   [ #3 Org & Methods Analyst ]
	

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
• Summary card is the first visible element on the results page — nothing appears above it
• Headline uses the user's first name from Step 1 of the form
• One-sentence result is dynamically generated using: total points, number of occupation matches, number of eligible states
• Threshold status indicator is green (above) or red (below) based on the user's actual points vs 65
• Four anchor boxes scroll smoothly to their target section when clicked
• Anchor box labels use the actual matched occupation names, not placeholder text
	

Priority: P1  |  Effort: M  |  Owner: Frontend + Backend (points/state count needed)

CURRENT STATE — THE PROBLEM
A tab switcher appears on the results page with two options: 'Skills Assessment' and 'Employer Sponsored'. The tool is built for skilled migration (GSM). Showing an employer sponsored tab implies coverage the tool does not yet have, and divides the user's attention at a critical moment.


ACTION REQUIRED
Required change: Remove the Employer Sponsored tab entirely.


The tool is scoped to the GSM skills assessment pathway. If employer sponsored pathways are added in future, they should be a separate flow — not a tab on the same results page. Keeping focus makes the product more authoritative and easier to build correctly.


Future consideration: if employer sponsored is added later, it should be a separate page or a clearly labelled 'beta' section — not a tab that implies parity with the main tool.
	

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
• No tab switcher appears on the results page
• All results shown are skills assessment pathway results only
• No reference to employer sponsored visa (482/494) anywhere on the results page
	

Priority: P0  |  Effort: S  |  Owner: Frontend

2.4  Occupation Card — Full Restructure
CURRENT STATE — THE PROBLEM
Each occupation card opens with a 3–4 line AI-generated paragraph. This is information-dense and hard to scan. Users need to identify their pathway in seconds, not read an essay. The key facts (visa pathway, assessing body, list status) are buried below the paragraph rather than leading the card.


REQUIRED — NEW OCCUPATION CARD STRUCTURE
The following is the exact layout required for each occupation card, from top to bottom. Build to this specification.


CARD STRUCTURE — READ TOP TO BOTTOM
	ROW 1 — OCCUPATION IDENTIFIER
[#1]  ICT Business Analyst   |   ANZSCO 261111   |   [ STRONG MATCH ] (green badge)
Note: Replace % score with label. Strong Match = dark green. Medium Match = amber. Weak Match = red.
	ROW 2 — POINTS SIGNAL (PROMINENT, ABOVE THE DATA TILES)
↑ 15 points above the 189 threshold   (threshold: 95 pts  |  your score: 90 pts)
Colour: green if above threshold, red if below. This is the most powerful single data point — make it prominent.
	ROW 3 — 4 DATA TILES (KEEP EXISTING DESIGN)
[ List Status: MLTSSL ]  [ 189 Min Points: 95 ]  [ Possibility: High ✓ ]  [ State Nomination: 6 states ]
Fix: Replace N/A with 'Not on 189 list' (red) if occupation is not on the 189 schedule. Never show bare N/A.
	ROW 4 — SKILL ASSESSING BODY (SINGLE PROMINENT TILE)
SKILL ASSESSING BODY:  ACS (Australian Computer Society)
This feeds directly into the paid document tool — make it clearly visible. It must link to the relevant assessing body's website.
	ROW 5 — WHY THIS OCCUPATION? (BULLET POINTS, NOT A PARAGRAPH)
Replace the AI paragraph with 3–4 structured bullet points:
* Your job title (Business Analyst) directly maps to ANZSCO 261111
* Your Bachelor of Business Analytics is a relevant qualification for ACS assessment
* Your Power BI and SQL experience aligns with ICT Business Analyst core duties
AI prompt guidance: generate exactly 3 bullet points. Each must reference a specific detail from the user's form inputs. No generic statements.
	ROW 6 — RISKS TO BE AWARE OF (EXPLICIT LABEL, NOT HIDDEN WARNING ICONS)
Rename from generic amber warning triangles to an explicit 'Risks to be aware of' heading.
* Your duties lean toward data/BI tools rather than full ICT system requirements gathering
This is your most differentiated feature — no other tool tells users where they might fail. Lean into it. Label it clearly.
	ROW 7 — PATHWAY SIGNALS (KEEP EXISTING)
Keep the existing pathway signals section as-is. The copy and formatting work well.
	ROW 8 — STATE & TERRITORY NOMINATION TABLE (KEEP EXISTING)
Keep the existing 190/491 × state matrix. It is excellent and unique. See Section 3.3 for the 'Best state for you' addition above this table.
	

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
• Card opens with occupation name, ANZSCO code, and match label (not percentage)
• Points signal is the second visible element, in green (above threshold) or red (below)
• N/A never appears in the 189 Min Points tile — replaced with 'Not on 189 list' or 'Data pending'
• 'Why this occupation?' section contains bullet points referencing specific user inputs, not a paragraph
• 'Risks to be aware of' is an explicit section heading — not icon-only warning flags
• All 8 rows appear in the order specified above
	

Priority: P1  |  Effort: M  |  Owner: Frontend + AI prompt engineering

2.5  Sticky CTA Replacement — Contextual Dual CTA
CURRENT STATE — THE PROBLEM
'Start Your Skill Assessment' is pinned permanently to the bottom of the screen from the moment the results page loads. It appears while the user is still reading and competes with the content on every scroll position. A user who has just started reading their first occupation card is not ready to act — showing the CTA here creates pressure, not conversion.


REQUIRED — CONTEXTUAL CTA STRATEGY


CTA BEHAVIOUR RULES
	Rule 1 — Hide CTA during reading
Do not show any sticky CTA while the user is scrolling through the points breakdown and occupation cards.
Rule 2 — Trigger CTA after reading
Detect when the user has scrolled past the bottom of the third occupation card. At that scroll depth — and only at that scroll depth — display the dual CTA section.
Rule 3 — Two options, not one
Display two side-by-side CTAs: a primary (paid document tool) and a secondary (free Studynash agent call). The secondary is the lead generation mechanism for Studynash.
	

CONTEXTUAL SECONDARY CTA COPY — VARIES BY TOP MATCH POSSIBILITY


Top match result
	Primary CTA (same always)
	Secondary CTA copy (changes)
	High possibility
	Prepare my skill assessment documents →
	You're in a strong position — book a free call to plan your next steps
	Medium possibility
	Prepare my skill assessment documents →
	Your pathway has complexity — a migration agent can significantly improve your chances
	Low possibility
	Prepare my skill assessment documents →
	Your situation may need a different strategy — speak to our agents for a personalised plan
	

FOR THE CTO
TECH NOTE — Scroll depth detection
Use an IntersectionObserver on the last occupation card container. When the bottom of that element enters the viewport, trigger the CTA display.
Example: const observer = new IntersectionObserver(([entry]) => { if (entry.isIntersecting) showDualCTA(); }, { threshold: 1.0 });
observer.observe(document.querySelector('#occupation-card-3'));
	

FOR THE CTO — STUDYNASH INTEGRATION
LEAD GEN NOTE — Secondary CTA behaviour
When user clicks 'Speak to a migration agent': (1) open a Calendly booking link or WhatsApp chat (configure URL in env var), (2) simultaneously fire a CRM event capturing the user's name, email (if available), occupation match, and points score as a warm lead in the Studynash pipeline.
Every user who clicks this CTA should appear in the Odoo pipeline as a New Lead with source = 'imminash results page'.
	

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
• No sticky CTA is visible while user is scrolling through points breakdown or occupation cards
• Dual CTA section appears after user scrolls past bottom of occupation card #3
• Primary CTA is always 'Prepare my skill assessment documents'
• Secondary CTA copy matches the user's top match possibility (High / Medium / Low)
• Secondary CTA click fires a CRM lead event before opening booking link
	

Priority: P0  |  Effort: M  |  Owner: Frontend + CRM integration

* Section heading: 'How to improve your score'
* Only show point categories where the user scored below maximum
* Sort by points available descending — biggest wins first
* Each row: category name, points available, how to get them, timeframe

DATA DEPENDENCY
This feature requires the backend to calculate available points per category as part of the results generation. The gap analysis table is derived from: max_points_per_category (static, from DHA table) minus user_points_per_category (calculated from form inputs).
No new AI call is needed — this is pure arithmetic on existing data.
	

Priority: P1  |  Effort: M  |  Owner: Backend + Frontend


3.2  Invitation Round Trend per Occupation
Currently the tool shows 'last invited at 95 points' as a single static data point. This is useful but incomplete — it doesn't tell the user whether getting in is becoming easier or harder over time. A trend indicator transforms this from a snapshot into a signal.


REQUIRED DISPLAY FORMAT


* Show last 3 invitation rounds for each occupation (189 schedule)
* Format: 'Recent 189 invitations: 95 pts → 95 pts → 90 pts'
* Trend arrow: green ↓ if cutoff is falling (easier to get in), red ↑ if rising (harder)
* Implementation: CSS-only mini bars or plain text — no chart library needed


FOR THE CTO — DATA REQUIREMENT
DATA DEPENDENCY
Requires a database table of historical invitation rounds per ANZSCO occupation code. Minimum fields: anzsco_code, round_date, points_cutoff, visa_subclass.
Source: DHA publishes invitation round data at homeaffairs.gov.au. This must be manually updated after each round or scraped via a scheduled job.
Fallback: if fewer than 3 rounds of data exist for an occupation, show whatever is available with a note: 'Limited invitation history for this occupation'.
	

Priority: P2  |  Effort: M  |  Owner: Backend (data) + Frontend (display)


3.3  State Recommendation Engine
The state/territory table already shows which states nominate each occupation. The missing step is telling the user which state is actually best for them, given their specific points score and occupation. This is a high-value insight that no other free tool provides.


REQUIRED — 'BEST STATE FOR YOU' RECOMMENDATION


* Position: directly above the state/territory nomination table
* Logic: find the state with the lowest current 190 nomination threshold that the user's points score already meets, for their matched occupation
* Output example: "Based on your 90 points and ICT Business Analyst occupation, South Australia is currently your strongest nomination option — their 190 threshold is 85 points and your occupation is on their priority list."
* Highlight the recommended state in the table with a distinct indicator (star, bold, or colour)


FOR THE CTO — DATA REQUIREMENT
DATA DEPENDENCY
Requires a database table of current state nomination thresholds per occupation per visa subclass. Fields: state, anzsco_code, visa_subclass (190/491), current_points_threshold, is_occupation_on_priority_list, last_updated.
This data must be updated when states publish new nomination criteria — typically at the start of each migration program year.
Fallback: if threshold data is not available for a state, exclude it from the recommendation and note 'Threshold data pending' in the state table.
	

Priority: P1  |  Effort: M  |  Owner: Backend (data) + Frontend


3.4  Re-Assessment Trigger & Reminder Email
Immigration journeys span years. A user who checks today may have materially different circumstances in 3–6 months — their visa may be expiring, they may be finishing a degree, or they may be expecting a promotion. Capturing a trigger date and sending a reminder at that point keeps users in the imminash ecosystem and creates a natural re-engagement moment for Studynash.


REQUIRED — TRIGGER CAPTURE UI (APPEARS AFTER DUAL CTA SECTION)


* Heading: 'When does your situation next change?'
* Present three options as selectable cards:
* My visa expires on [date picker] — remind me 90 days before
* I am completing my studies in [month/year picker] — remind me then
* I am expecting a promotion or role change in [X months selector]
* On selection: capture user email (if not already captured) and the trigger date
* Email sent at trigger date: personalised, links back to imminash with pre-filled form option


FOR THE CTO
DATA DEPENDENCY & TECH NOTE
Requires: (1) email capture field if user has not signed in yet, (2) a scheduled job or email automation (e.g. Mailchimp, SendGrid, or similar) that fires at the trigger date.
CRM note: each trigger capture should also create or update a Studynash CRM record with the trigger date — this feeds a long-term nurture sequence.
	

Priority: P2  |  Effort: L  |  Owner: Backend + Email automation

05
	Why We Are Building in This Order
Strategic context for prioritisation decisions
	

This section exists so the CTO understands the business rationale behind each priority tier. Build order is not arbitrary — it follows the user's critical path and the product's conversion funnel.


P0 — Fix the Leaks First
P0 items are changes where the current product is actively losing users or revenue. They are not enhancements — they are repairs.


* The magic link sign-in is losing users at the highest-intent moment in the flow. This is a direct revenue leak.
* The sticky CTA is creating pressure during reading, which reduces trust. Trust is what converts users to pay.
* N/A in data tiles and the missing employer sponsored removal both signal that the product is unfinished — users who see these signals are less likely to pay for the document tool.
* The value page gap means users are being asked to pay with no context. This is the most direct barrier to revenue.
Ship all P0 items before adding any new features.


P1 — Deepen the Value Proposition
P1 items make the product meaningfully better than anything else in the market. They are features users will talk about.


* The personalised summary card and occupation card restructure make the results page scannable in under 10 seconds. Speed of comprehension is directly correlated with conversion.
* The points gap analysis is the feature most likely to be shared on WhatsApp and Reddit by international student communities. Shareability drives organic acquisition.
* The state recommendation engine is unique — no other tool tells users which specific state is best for them. This creates genuine, defensible differentiation.


P2 — Build the Moat
P2 items are what makes this product hard to copy over time. They require data and infrastructure that a competitor starting today cannot replicate.


* Invitation round trend data requires a historical database that takes time to build. Starting now means a 12-month head start over any competitor.
* Re-assessment reminders create a long-term relationship with users — this is the beginning of a CRM-driven retention strategy for both imminash and Studynash.


The Studynash Connection
imminash is not just a standalone product — it is a lead generation and trust-building machine for Studynash. Every design decision should be evaluated through this lens:


* The secondary CTA on the results page feeds warm, pre-qualified leads directly into the Studynash pipeline
* Users who book a call have already seen their points score, matched occupations, and risk flags — they arrive at the call informed, which means the consultation is higher quality and conversion to paid Studynash services is higher
* The document tool itself (the skill assessment workspace) is the bridge — users who pay for documents have already self-identified as serious PR applicants. They are the highest-value potential clients for Studynash's full service offering.
